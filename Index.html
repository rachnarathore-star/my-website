A football game, commonly referred to as soccer in some countries, is a team sport played between two teams of 11 players each. The objective of the game is to score more goals than the opposing team by getting the ball into the opponent's goal. A goal is scored when the entire ball crosses the goal line between the goalposts and beneath the crossbar.

Basic Rules and Regulations of Football

The Field of Play
  1. The game is played on a rectangular field with dimensions typically between 100-110 meters in length and 64-75 meters in width, depending on competition standards.
  2. The field has two goalposts (each at the end of the field) and a center circle in the middle.
Players
  1. Each team has 11 players, including 1 Goalkeeper whose role is to protect the goal.
The Ball
  1. A standard football (soccer ball) is typically made of leather or other synthetic materials, with a circumference between 68-70 cm and weighing 400-450 grams.
The Objective
  1. A goal is scored when the entire ball crosses the goal line between the two goalposts and beneath the crossbar.
Match Duration
  1. A standard match consists of two 45-minute halves, with a 15-minute halftime break in between.
  2. A standard match consists of two 45-minute halves, with a 15-minute halftime break in between.
Offside Rule
  1. A player is considered offside if they are nearer to the opponent’s goal line than the ball and the second-last opponent when the ball is passed to them (unless they are in their own half).
Fouls and Misconduct
  1. Actions such as kicking, tripping, pushing, or holding an opponent are considered fouls.
  2. Misconduct can lead to:
  3. A free kick, penalty kick, or yellow card (warning).
  4. A red card, which results in the player being sent off for serious or repeated offenses.
Free Kicks & Penalty Kicks
  1. A free kick is awarded to a team if a player commits a foul outside the penalty area.
  2. A penalty kick is awarded if a direct free kick offense is committed inside the defending team’s penalty area.
Throw-Ins
  1. A throw-in is awarded when the ball crosses the touchline (sideline), and the team that did not touch the ball last performs the throw.
Goal Kicks and Corner Kicks
  1. A goal kick is awarded if the attacking team last touched the ball before it went over the goal line.
  2. A corner kick is awarded if the defending team last touched the ball before it went over the goal line.

Regulations and Equipment

Player Equipment

  • Players are required to wear:
  • A team jersey/shirt.
  • Shorts.
  • Shin guards.
  • Socks.
  • Soccer cleats/boots.

Ball Specifications

  • A properly inflated football must adhere to FIFA or competition standards, with the proper size and weight.

Goalposts

  • Standard goalposts are 7.32 meters wide and 2.44 meters high.

Key Terms in Football

  • Goal: Scoring by getting the ball into the opponent’s net.
  • Offside: A rule violation where an attacking player is nearer to the opponent’s goal line than both the ball and the second-last defender when the ball is passed to them.
  • Free Kick: A kick awarded to a team after a foul; can be direct (toward goal) or indirect (requires another player to touch).
  • Penalty Kick: A direct shot at goal from the penalty spot due to a foul in the penalty area.
  • Corner Kick: Awarded when the defending team last touches the ball before it crosses the goal line, outside the goal.
  • Yellow/Red Card: Yellow signifies a warning; red results in ejection from the match.
  • Kickoff: The starting play or restart after a goal is scored.
